# Innovations of Han Dynasty China

## Technology
- Paper (invented during Eastern Han by Cai Lun in 105 CE)
- Seismograph (Zhang Heng's device in 132 CE)
- Improvements to the crossbow
- Wheelbarrow
- Deep drilling for salt and natural gas
- Steel production techniques
- Improvements to the plow

## Science
- Astronomical observations and calendars
- Mathematical advances (Nine Chapters on the Mathematical Art)
- Medical knowledge (Huangdi Neijing - Yellow Emperor's Inner Canon)
- Geography and cartography
- Natural philosophy and cosmology

## Transportation
- Expansion of the Silk Road network
- Road construction and maintenance systems
- Canal building and waterway management
- Improvements to ships and navigation

## Government
- Civil service examination system
- Merit-based bureaucracy
- Census and population registration
- Standardized weights and measures
- Imperial university system

## Literature and Arts
- Development of the historical narrative tradition (Sima Qian's Records of the Grand Historian)
- Fu poetry form
- Historical writing as political instruction
- Artistic techniques in pottery, bronze, and lacquerware

## Economics
- Standardized currency (wuzhu coins)
- State monopolies on salt and iron
- Banking and credit systems
- Agricultural innovations (crop rotation, new crops)
- Water management for agriculture

## Philosophy and Religion
- Establishment of Confucianism as state ideology
- Integration of Confucian, Daoist, and Legalist ideas
- Development of philosophical syncretism
- Buddhist introduction (during Eastern Han)

## Architecture
- Palace and temple construction techniques
- Urban planning principles
- Defensive architecture (Great Wall improvements)
- Bridge construction

## Agriculture
- Introduction of new crops from Central Asia via Silk Road
- Improved irrigation systems
- Agricultural manuals and techniques
- Animal husbandry improvements
- Farming tools innovations
