# Social Systems of Han Dynasty China

## Government
### Imperial System
- Emperor ruled under the Mandate of Heaven
- Centralized bureaucratic state
- Complex administrative system with multiple levels
- Imperial court with elaborate ceremonial protocols

### Bureaucracy
- Civil service examination system (though not fully developed until later)
- Merit-based appointments (increasingly after Wu Di)
- Nine-rank system for classifying officials
- Specialized departments for different functions (agriculture, military, etc.)
- Regional administrators appointed from center

### Legal System
- Legalist foundation with Confucian overlay
- Hierarchical justice system
- Corporal punishment and capital punishment
- Family-based legal responsibilities

## Economics
### Agriculture
- Primary economic activity
- Equal-field system (juntian) under some emperors
- Irrigation and flood control projects
- State involvement in agricultural planning

### Trade and Commerce
- Silk Road trade expansion
- Internal trade networks
- State monopolies on salt and iron (at times)
- Commercial taxation

### Manufacturing
- Paper production (invented during Han)
- Bronze and iron working
- Silk production
- Pottery and ceramics
- Mining operations

## Family Structure
### Confucian Family Model
- Patriarchal extended families
- Filial piety as core value
- Ancestor veneration
- Strict generational hierarchy
- Arranged marriages for social advancement

### Social Mobility
- Possible through education and civil service
- Scholar-official class (shi)
- Peasant, artisan, merchant hierarchy (shinonggongshang)

## Gender Roles
### Confucian Ideals
- **Men**: Expected to pursue education, government service, provide for family
- **Women**: Emphasized virtue, proper behavior, management of household
- **Three obediences**: Women to father, husband, and son
- **Wu chang (five constants)**: Applied differently to men and women

### Reality vs. Ideals
- Elite women could wield significant influence
- Women managed household finances and domestic production
- Some women achieved power as empresses dowager

## Education System
### Confucian Education
- Focus on classical texts: Five Classics and later Four Books
- Emphasis on moral cultivation
- Preparation for civil service
- Private and public schools
- Imperial University established during Han

### Scholar-Official Class
- Education as path to government service
- Literati as social elite
- Cultural transmission through educated class

## Military System
### Organization
- Professional army
- Conscript and volunteer forces
- Frontier troops and garrisons
- Horse breeding programs
- Military colonies on frontiers

### Technology
- Crossbow improvements
- Cavalry development
- Fortification techniques
- Military engineering

## Social Classes
### Traditional Hierarchy
- **Scholar-Officials (shi)**: Highest social rank
- **Farmers (nong)**: Valued for productive work
- **Artisans (gong)**: Skilled manufacturing
- **Merchants (shang)**: Lowest rank, though often wealthy

### Additional Categories
- Imperial family and nobility
- Slaves and bonded laborers
- Monks and religious practitioners (especially Buddhist in later Han)
- Foreigners and minorities with different statuses
