# Innovations of Maya Civilization

## Mathematics
- Concept of zero as a placeholder and concept
- Vigesimal (base-20) numerical system
- Advanced calculations for astronomical predictions
- Complex calendar calculations and computations
- Mathematical concepts for architectural planning

## Writing System
- Hieroglyphic writing system with over 800 glyphs
- Logographic and syllabic elements
- Codices and monumental inscriptions
- Historical and ceremonial record keeping
- Literary traditions and mythological texts

## Calendar Systems
- Tzolk'in (260-day sacred calendar)
- Haab' (365-day solar calendar)
- Long Count calendar for historical dating
- Calendar Round combining Tzolk'in and Haab'
- Astronomical calculations for eclipse prediction

## Astronomy
- Precise observations of celestial bodies
- Predictions of eclipses and planetary movements
- Venus tables and planetary cycles
- Architectural alignment with celestial events
- Complex astronomical knowledge encoded in monuments

## Architecture
- Pyramid construction techniques
- Corbelled arch construction
- Elaborate temple-pyramids and palaces
- Urban planning with ceremonial centers
- Advanced masonry and stucco work

## Engineering
- Water management systems (reservoirs, canals)
- Agricultural terracing
- Raised field construction (chinampas-like systems)
- Road networks (sacbeob)
- Construction techniques for tropical climate

## Arts
- Advanced pottery and ceramic techniques
- Jade carving and precious stone work
- Stucco sculpture and architectural decoration
- Murals and pictorial art
- Textile and feather work

## Agriculture
- Slash-and-burn farming (milpa system)
- Crop rotation and soil management
- Domestication of numerous plants
- Water management for agriculture
- Intensive farming techniques

## Medicine
- Advanced knowledge of medicinal plants
- Surgical techniques
- Dental modification and care
- Understanding of anatomy and diseases
- Specialized healing practitioners

## Political Organization
- Divine kingship system
- Complex diplomatic relationships
- Vassalage and tribute systems
- Alliance networks between city-states
- Ceremonial and political center planning
