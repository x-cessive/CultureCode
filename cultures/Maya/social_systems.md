# Social Systems of Maya Civilization

## Government
### Divine Kingship (K'uhul Ajaw)
- Rulers considered divine intermediaries between human and supernatural realms
- Hereditary monarchy with complex succession systems
- Divine kings performed crucial religious and ceremonial functions
- Title "k'uhul ajaw" meaning "holy lord"

### City-States (Ajawlel)
- Independent political units centered on ceremonial centers
- Complex system of alliances, vassalage, and warfare
- Diplomatic marriages and tribute systems
- Warfare for captives, tribute, and political dominance

### Administration
- Noble class (almehenob) as regional administrators
- Scribes (t檀nim) as record keepers and officials
- Regional governors and military commanders
- Specialized functionaries for trade and tribute

## Economics
### Agriculture
- Slash-and-burn agriculture (milpa system)
- Raised field systems in wetlands
- Intensive farming of maize, beans, squash (the "Three Sisters")
- Cacao as both crop and currency

### Trade
- Long-distance trade networks across Mesoamerica
- Obsidian, jade, quetzal feathers, cacao as luxury goods
- Marketplace systems in urban centers
- Professional merchant class (pochteca in some regions)

### Craft Production
- Specialized artisans for luxury goods
- Obsidian working and tool production
- Jade carving and precious stone work
- Pottery and textile production

## Family Structure
### Kinship Systems
- Extended family groups with patrilineal elements
- Royal dynasties traced through both male and female lines
- Complex marriage systems including royal intermarriage
- Household-based economic units

### Social Hierarchy
- Royal families at apex
- Noble class (almehenob) with hereditary status
- Professional classes (scribes, artisans, warriors)
- Commoners (macehualli in some areas)
- Captives and slaves from warfare

## Gender Roles
### Royal Women
- Could rule as regents or independently in some cases
- Important roles in dynastic alliances through marriage
- Participation in bloodletting rituals
- Burial with rich grave goods indicating status

### Common Women
- Primary agricultural and domestic labor
- Textile production was important specialized activity
- Child-rearing and household management
- Participation in market activities

### Men
- Warfare and military service
- Agricultural labor and craft production
- Religious and ceremonial roles (especially bloodletting)
- Administrative and scribal functions

## Education System
### Elite Education
- Training in writing and calendar systems
- Religious and ceremonial knowledge
- Astronomical observation techniques
- Political and administrative skills

### Craft Specialization
- Apprentice systems for specialized skills
- Artistic and architectural training
- Technical knowledge transmission

## Religious System
### Polytheistic Pantheon
- Complex pantheon with multiple deities
- Maize god, sun god, rain god (Chaac), death god among important deities
- Deified ancestors and patron gods of cities
- Ritual calendar with ceremonial cycles

### Ceremonial Centers
- Pyramids and temples as ritual centers
- Ball courts for ceremonial and political purposes
- Astronomical observation platforms
- Elite residential complexes

## Social Classes
- **K'uhul Ajaw (Divine Kings)**: Supreme political and religious authority
- **Nobility (Almehenob)**: Regional lords, administrators, high officials
- **Scribes (T檀nim)**: Literate professionals, record keepers, artists
- **Merchants and Artisans**: Specialized traders and craftspeople
- **Commoners**: Farmers, laborers, soldiers
- **Captives and Slaves**: War captives and bonded individuals
