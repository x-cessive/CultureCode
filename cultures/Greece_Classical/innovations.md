# Innovations of Classical Greece

## Philosophy
- Development of Western philosophical tradition (Socrates, Plato, Aristotle)
- Socratic method of inquiry
- Systematic examination of ethics, metaphysics, and epistemology
- Creation of philosophical schools (Academy, Lyceum, Stoa)
- Logic and reasoning as tools for understanding truth

## Political Systems
- Democracy (rule by the people) in Athens
- Constitutional government
- Civic participation and public debate
- Legal systems based on written laws
- Concept of citizenship and civic duty

## Literature
- Tragedy (Aeschylus, Sophocles, Euripides)
- Comedy (Aristophanes)
- Epic poetry (Homer's influence continued)
- History as a discipline (Herodotus, Thucydides)
- Rhetoric and oratory

## Science and Mathematics
- Mathematical proofs and axiomatic systems (Euclid's work built on earlier Greek mathematicians)
- Geometry and number theory
- Early scientific method and empirical observation
- Medical ethics and practice (Hippocratic tradition)
- Astronomy and cosmological models

## Architecture
- Classical orders: Doric, Ionic, Corinthian
- Parthenon as architectural masterpiece
- Public buildings (theaters, stoas, bouleuteria)
- Urban planning principles
- Engineering advances in construction

## Arts
- Realistic sculpture and idealized human forms
- Red and black figure pottery techniques
- Theatrical performance and dramatic conventions
- Musical theory and practice
- Artistic representations of mythological and historical themes

## Military Technology
- Phalanx formation warfare
- Advanced ship design for naval warfare (triremes)
- Military engineering and siege warfare techniques
- Professional military training

## Education
- Systematic education for citizenship
- Paideia (comprehensive education system)
- Physical and intellectual training balance
- Gymnasium system for physical education
- Mentor-student relationships (pedagogy)
