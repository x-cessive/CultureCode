# Innovations of Islamic Golden Age

## Mathematics
- Algebra (from Al-Khwarizmi's work)
- Algorithm (named after Al-Khwarizmi)
- Decimal fractions and decimal point notation
- Improvements to the numerical system
- Spherical trigonometry
- Geometric patterns and tessellations

## Medicine
- Comprehensive medical encyclopedias (Canon of Medicine by Avicenna)
- Surgical techniques and instruments
- Hospital organization and medical education
- Pharmacology and drug preparation
- Anatomical studies and medical ethics
- Public health systems and quarantine practices

## Astronomy
- Accurate astronomical tables (zijes)
- Improvement of astrolabe and other instruments
- Accurate measurement of the Earth's circumference
- Star catalogues
- Observatories for systematic observation
- Correction of Ptolemaic models

## Chemistry
- Systematic experimental approach to chemistry
- Laboratory techniques and apparatus
- Distillation, crystallization, and other chemical processes
- Classification of chemical substances
- Development of alchemy into chemistry
- Practical applications in metallurgy and manufacturing

## Optics
- Correct understanding of vision (light rays from objects to eyes)
- Experiments with lenses and mirrors
- Camera obscura improvements
- Principles of reflection and refraction
- Optical instruments and measurements

## Philosophy
- Integration of Aristotelian philosophy with Islamic theology
- Works of Al-Farabi, Avicenna (Ibn Sina), Averroes (Ibn Rushd)
- Preservation and commentary on Greek philosophical works
- Development of Islamic philosophical traditions
- Influence on Medieval European scholasticism

## Geography and Cartography
- Accurate maps of the known world
- Geographic encyclopedias
- Mathematical geography
- Travel accounts and geographic descriptions
- Navigation techniques and tools

## Literature
- Development of Arabic literary traditions
- Poetry and prose innovations
- Travel literature (like Ibn Battuta's accounts)
- Historical writing and source criticism
- Translation and preservation of classical texts

## Technology and Engineering
- Water management and irrigation systems
- Windmills and watermills
- Mechanical devices and automata
- Improvements to papermaking from China
- Urban planning and architecture
- Timekeeping devices (water clocks, astrolabes)

## Institutions
- University system (al-Azhar, etc.)
- Hospital system with specialized departments
- Library and information preservation
- Translation movement preserving Greek works
- Scientific academies and research centers
