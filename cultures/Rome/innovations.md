# Innovations of Ancient Rome

## Legal Systems
- Roman Law (corpus juris civilis)
- Legal concepts: innocent until proven guilty, burden of proof
- Property law and contract law
- Administrative law for governing large territories
- Legal profession and legal education

## Engineering and Architecture
- Concrete technology (opus caementicium)
- Aqueducts for water supply
- Road systems (viae) connecting the empire
- Arch and dome construction techniques
- Central heating systems (hypocaust)
- Advanced bridge building

## Military Technology
- Legionary organization and tactics
- Standardized equipment and training
- Siege warfare techniques and machines
- Engineering corps for construction and warfare
- Naval innovations (liburnae, corvus)

## Administration
- Bureaucratic systems for large territories
- Census and taxation systems
- Postal system (cursus publicus)
- Standardized currency and weights/measures
- Provincial governance systems

## Urban Planning
- Grid-based city planning
- Public sanitation systems
- Public entertainment venues (amphitheaters, circuses)
- Fire and police services
- City infrastructure (sewers, water, roads)

## Infrastructure
- Extensive road networks (over 250,000 miles)
- Bridges and tunnels
- Ports and harbors
- Water distribution systems
- Trade and commerce facilitation

## Construction Techniques
- Advanced masonry techniques
- Crane and lifting technology
- Large-scale project management
- Use of standardized components
- Maintenance and repair systems

## Financial Systems
- Banking and credit systems
- Insurance for maritime trade
- Complex accounting methods
- Standardized currency across territories
- Public and private financial institutions

## Medicine
- Military medical corps
- Surgical techniques and instruments
- Public health and sanitation
- Hospital systems
- Medical specialization

## Agriculture
- Crop rotation systems
- Advanced irrigation
- New farming tools and techniques
- Large-scale agricultural estates
- Food storage and distribution systems
